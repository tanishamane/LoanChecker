Frequently Asked Questions

What is the credit score range? ▼
Credit scores typically range from 300 (lowest) to 900 (highest). A higher score means better chances of loan approval.
How is my Credit Score determined? ▼
Your score is calculated based on repayment history, credit utilization, length of credit history, types of credit, and recent enquiries. Payment history and utilization are most important.
Who calculates credit scores? ▼
Credit bureaus like CIBIL, Experian, Equifax, and CRIF High Mark maintain and calculate your score based on lender data.
Does having too many credit cards affect a credit score? ▼
Having many cards does not hurt your score as long as you manage them well; late payments and high utilization can lower your score.
How do credit scores change? ▼
Scores update as you pay bills, take new credit, close loans, or apply for new credit cards. Major changes in usage, missed payments, or reported defaults can affect your score.
Why don't I have a credit score? ▼
You may not have a score if you’ve never taken a loan or used a credit card, or if your accounts are very recent or inactive.
How can I improve my Credit Score? ▼
Pay all bills and EMIs on time, keep utilization below 30%, avoid frequent new credit applications, and regularly monitor your credit report for errors.
